Tequila and Mescal

...

Tequila is a true symbol of Mexico. It is as free and passionate in its essence as the Mexican soul itself!

Tequila and mescal are strong alcoholic beverages. Their creation is connected to the Spaniards and the Portuguese, who brought the knowledge of distillation to the Americas.

The common ancestor of tequila and mescal is pulque (a brewed juice of blue agave), which has existed in Mexico since ancient times.

In 1656, the city of Tequila was founded. A hundred years later, in 1758, the noble and wealthy Cuervo family owned extensive land near Tequila. In 1795, Jaze Maria Guadeloupe de Cuervo sought special permission from the government to produce "Mescal Vino de Tequila,” which can be translated as “Mescal wines from Tequila.” This was the separation of mezcal and tequila — just as authentic cognac, as opposed to other brandies, can only be produced in Cognac, France, the right to be called tequila was assigned to mescal made using unique technology in the state of Jalisco, near the city of Tequila.

Tequila is made from blue agave (Agave tequiliana). The essence of agave is to live to the fullest and enjoy life, good company, food, and sex. It is a plant of radical hedonism.

In particular, the blue agave brings out all the hidden and unfulfilled desires. Tequila is for those who always tend to be too good and do "the right thing,” depriving themselves of the joys and pleasures of life.

All agaves are about dancing and singing. They stimulate the desire to move and experience, to let go of limitations, and to discover your wish for happiness and passion.
