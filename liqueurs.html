The World of Liqueurs

The term "liqueur" comes from the Latin verb liquefacere — to dissolve, which gives us an idea of the preparation method — dissolving various ingredients in alcohol. The term "liqueur" was introduced by French monks, who mastered the art of distillation and were well-versed in medicinal plants.

Monks knew perfectly well that herbs and roots have healing properties. They also understood that a concentrated solution of alcohol was a powerful antiseptic and a powerful extractant; it could be quickly absorbed into the blood and promoted rapid absorption of all extractive substances contained in it. Since many alcohol-based drugs were bitter, honey was added. This is how the first elixirs and healing tinctures appeared, which the monks called "liqueur" in Latin.
