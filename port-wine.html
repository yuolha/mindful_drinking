Port Wine

Port is a brilliant wine. It has many semantic layers and many different Port wines, and each embodies something of its own. To cut a long story short, I will explain the basic principles and offer you to analyze your favorite Port wine yourself.

Again, it will be easier for me to refer here to the theory of Human Design, according to which a person is woven from 3 groups of Great Energy Flows: the Collective (Shiva, the Destroyer), the Tribal (Vishnu, the Keeper), and the Individual (Brahma, the Creator). Thus, the principle of the Trinity is reflected in us. You may use the Christian understanding of the Trinity principle (the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit). Still, the Hindu philosophy is more accurate and thorough in describing those things. These Great Energies are mixed in each of us in unique proportions and interact with each other, determining the uniqueness of each human being. This is a very brief description of the way things are. The Human Design theory provides a deep analysis of these energies. Please refer to it if needed.

The production of Port wine makes it possible to create drinks in which these three types of Great Energies are present. At first, future Port wine is aged in huge oak vats (the Collective Energy), and then the technologist's decision will determine whether the drink will mature further in smaller barrels (the Tribal Energy) or bottles (The Individual Energy) or in barrels and then in bottles.

Port is bottled poetry. (Peter Lanberg)

Aging in barrels involves the oxidation of wine due to the air penetrating through the wood’s pores. So the man of the Tribal type develops and is mainly formed under the influence of the close circle of people (the “tribe"). Then this wine is bottled when it has used up its potential for “development" and maturation.

Ripening in tightly corked bottles occurs due to the internal potential of wine, if it has any — this is the fate of every Individual type, to do his own thing, to dance to his music. This is how vintage Port wines are produced.

So every Port wine has its aging terms in specific containers and its ripening methods, which determine the ratio of 3 types of the Great Energies in the final product. Ingenious, right? But that's not all!

There are also male and female aspects of the Trinity principle. For example, Christians worship the male ones only, while the Hindus have the female ones: Adi Shakti — the Primal Force (the Collective), Lakshmi — the Prosperity (the Tribal), and Saraswati — the Knowledge and Art (the Individual). Those are the wives of Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma, respectively. They have their representation in port wine also. Filtered Ports are those in which only the male aspect is present; unfiltered Ports have both male and female aspects.

So grab a bottle of your favorite Port wine, read the label, and you will know precisely how the Three Great Energies dance in it.
