Vodka

No one knows where vodka originally came from. Russia, Poland, and even Sweden claim to be their true country of origin. Vodka is the most popular strong alcoholic drink throughout the world. Let's try to figure out why.

Vodka has entered the lives of people of different income levels, professions, and lifestyles. They drink it when they feel good and even more so when feeling bad.

Vodka is made mainly from grain (wheat, rye), but there may be also present some impurities of potatoes, beets, and molasses. The fewer impurities vodka contains, the better, and the more expensive it costs.

My analysis will be based on the pure type of vodka, but it may also apply to schnapps and moonshine.

"Is it vodka?" Margarita asked weakly. The cat jumped up in his seat with indignation. "I beg pardon, my queen," he rasped, "Would I ever allow myself to offer vodka to a lady? This is pure alcohol!" (Mikhail Bulgakov, The Master and Margarita)

Perhaps they have been through a trauma or a shock that knocked them off their feet... If vodka is a favorite drink, it might be helpful to think about what gives a sense of security and stability.
