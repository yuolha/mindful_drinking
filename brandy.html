Brandy

Indeed, cognac is the most famous type of brandy. Although cognac is always brandy, brandy is not always cognac.

Brandy combines a vast number of strong drinks, which can be divided into three groups, reflecting different aspects of a person's growing up:
1. Grape brandy is represented by cognac from the Cognac city, Poitou-Charentes, in France. Many other grape brandies are produced using cognac technology, such as Greek brandy Metaxa, South African brandies, Armenian brandies, American brandies, Moldavian brandies, Bulgarian brandies, Portuguese brandies, sherry brandy, Armagnac, vignac, and more.
2. Brandy from grape pomace (chacha, grappa, turkeypalinka…) resonates with depression, a natural companion of an awakening person. Here I want to refer to Jim Carrie, known for suffering from depression for quite a while. In one of his interviews, he made a great point by comparing depression with the role a person is tired of playing. It is the state that signals you to return to your true self.
3. Numerous fruit brandies. The type of fruit gives brandy its unique essence:
• Calvados is made of apples (Malus domestica or Malus pumila), representing doubts regarding a life partner and family life: am I mature enough to start a family? Is my partner the ONE? Do I love my partner? Does my partner love me? Apples are for those who have incredibly high standards for marriage; thus, there is a chance that they won't be able to achieve them.
• Kirschwasser is made of cherries (Cerasus vulgaris). Cherries are for those who lack trust, balance, and strength. It is a remedy to cope with extreme stress and fear of losing control and mentally and emotionally broken.
• Slivovitz is an unbelievable plum brandy (Prunus domestica), which in its essence, helps to deal with minds, flooded with shocking, antisocial, unwanted, and dangerous thoughts. Plums are also suitable for those prone to violence and obsessive, compulsive, and ritualistic behavior.
• Poire Williams is made of pears (Pyrus communis) connected with heart and digestive functions. On the psychological level, it represents those with a hard time mentally and emotionally processing reality.
• Framboise is made of raspberries (Rubus idaeus) that help treat the aftermath of trauma and shock.
• Eau de vie de pêche is made of peaches (Prunus persica), which are closely related to plums and share pretty much the same themes in their essence, but not at that scale.
• Pálinka is made of a fruit mix (may include apple, plum, pear, cherry, or apricot). Apricots (Prunus armeniaca) are also grouped with plums and are used to deal with the same conditions.

As you may see, the fruit brandies mentioned above are made of fruits of the Rose family. It is all about love and relationships. People, who need remedies of the Rose family, have a very naive idea about love and marriage. They are demanding and think that if somebody loves them honestly, they are supposed to do everything for them. It is about all the work that needs to be done by both partners so that relationships work. It is about the balance of giving and receiving, about an ability to love oneself to love somebody else. As you may have noticed, people who are into fruit brandies require maturity in love and marriage. Fruit brandies also reflect the darkest illusions and charms one may experience on the way to true adulthood.

Cognac is the liquor of the gods. (Victor Hugo)

Grape brandy is a "distilled" wine. If we apply this to a person, we may say that brandy represents a process of deconditioning, when one throws off the shackles of anyone else's influences, when a person “distills” ONESELF.

Oak barrels are used for brandy aging and are a perfect choice because of their physical and metaphysical properties.

Oak (Quercus robur) represents a natural leader responsible for family and society and strongly desires to benefit everyone.

Brandy is a drink about destiny, about that part of the Universe for which a mature person takes responsibility, cherishes, and develops. And sees the meaning in it.

My favorite composition by Malice Mizer is "Regret." Regrets are something we often experience while maturing—a perfect theme song for brandies.
