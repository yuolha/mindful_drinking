Absinthe

Absinthe has a history of bans because its main component (wormwood) contains thujone. This substance has a psychotropic effect and may cause side effects. Absinthe was banned from selling in most European countries and the US. The drink was officially legalized when the thujone content was set to 10 mg/l. Still, not all manufacturers stick to this norm. For example, some say you may still find absinthe with a thujone content as high as 100 mg/l. So, be mindful and always read the label carefully.

I have respected this controversial drink since my student days. I remember our bohemian-shabby rented apartment, candles, green absinthe with burning sugar...

Some of my friends would lie on the floor, some on the sofa, and we all would wait for the "green fairy.” We then drank absinthe thoughtfully and enchanted, immersed in ourselves. Well, as we should. That was one of those evenings that remain in my memory forever.

The main component of absinthe is wormwood (Artemisia absinthium), which is the guide to the nooks and crannies of an injured human soul. Why would creative and sensitive people love absinthe? Because it is just like them.

Wormwood represents a loner, immersed in themself, avoiding contact with others. The reason for such behavior lies in the experience of pain and abuse, suppression of one’s will, and individuality by people they used to trust. So it's better to keep their distance now. There may be a tendency to psychosis and paranoia. Episodes of active aggression (including physical) are the primary method of self-defense for such people.

This is the essence of wormwood that makes absinthe so intriguing and somewhat dangerous at the same time. Oscar Wilde figured it out just right.

After the first glass, you see things as you wish they were. After the second, you see things as they are not. Finally, you see things as they really are, and that is the most horrible thing in the world. (Oscar Wilde)

Thoughtful and strictly dosed use of absinthe as a medicine may relieve those who resonate with the essence of wormwood. Excessive and recreational use may sometimes lead to psychotic and aggressive reactions. This must be taken into account. I warned you, so don't say you didn't know.

Absinthe must be consumed slowly, with a great deal of attention to all the emotions and/or memories and/or images that may arise in your consciousness. I do not advise you to drink absinthe with people you don't trust. This drink requires a certain degree of intimacy, the ability to relax, and to let go. Having someone who will give you the perfect support and understanding is good.

Suppose you prefer absinthe over any other alcoholic beverage and drink it often. In that case, consider meeting a counselor to get a deeper insight into what stands behind this preference.
