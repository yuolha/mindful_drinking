Madeira Wine

One of the reasons I love Portugal is the excellent wines. It is a country of unique and noble wines like Port Wine and Madeira Wine.

When Italian sailors in the IV century and then the Portuguese in the XV century independently discovered an island in the Atlantic Ocean northwest of the coast of Africa, they were struck by the exuberant green vegetation covering the entire island’s surface. This wooded island, called Madeira (in Portuguese “madeira,” in Spanish “madera” — forest, wood), then gave its name to the original fortified wine.

Soon, however, some of the wild forests were replaced by cultivated sugar cane plantations from Sicily and the Malvasia grapevine, delivered from Crete on the direct instructions of Prince Henry the Navigator. Madeira’s wine is mentioned in Shakes peare's play "Henry IV” when it’s said that Falstaff sold his soul to the devil on Good Friday "for Madeira's mug and a cold capon leg."

Madeira is made from the wine grown on a dormant volcano. In addition, the wine material for Madeira is aged for quite a long time at high temperatures, which gives it a particular color, taste properties, and a unique effect on the psycho-emotional sphere.

Madeira is a wine that treats disappointments. It is a medicine for deep emotional wounds resulting from our illusions. Madeira brings warmth and fills in emotional "voids”; it helps to move on.

The bitterer the disappointment, the sweeter your Madeira wine should be.

Madeira is a wine like no other. It is fine wine in extremis. Heat and air, both the sworn enemies of most wines and winemakers, conspire to turn madeira into one of the most enthralling of the world's wines as well as the most resilient. Wines from the 19th and even the 18th centuries still retain an ethereal, youthful gloss, even after spending what is, in wine terms, an eon in cask and bottle. Having gone through this extreme and often an extensive ageing process, madeira is virtually indestructible. Once the cork is removed, the wine comes to no harm, even if the bottle is left on ullage for months, even for years on end. If ever there was a wine to take away with you to a desert island, this is it.

Madeira wine is one of many winemaking wonders of Portugal, but it is undoubtedly unique and unforgettable.
