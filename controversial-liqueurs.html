Controversial Liqueurs

I find some liqueurs controversial concerning their effect on the human psyche. There’s no reason to undermine their exceptional benefits to the body, but I would be cautious about using them regularly.

Becherovka and Jägermeister were conceived as gastric medicines. Their full recipes are kept secret. However, some conclusions can be drawn from the publicly known components and, in general, from their effect on consciousness. These liqueurs represent the relationship between "I and the tribe" (the closest circle of people); they are about personal will. They act as dope to assert the will of a person. These liqueurs tend to strengthen the will until they replace it.

Just like Becherovka and Jägermeister help the body digest food, a personal will is an instrument to “digest” whatever life offers. So, I suggest being mindful of the diet and the ability to make your way in life without relying too much on external assistance.

Aperol may be used to lower excessive bursts of libido, though it is healthier to eat tofu, for example. Otherwise, Aperol may harm your sexuality if consumed regularly.

And finally, Curaçao. It contains nutmeg — Myristica fragrans — corresponding to the feeling of complete loss and insecurity. Nutmeg should always be used with caution to avoid the development of narcolepsy. We recall that alcohol phenomenally potentializes (that is, reveals the essence) what gets into it. For this reason, I highly recommend being cautious with this liqueur.
