The history of gin dates back to the 11th century, when monks in Dutch monasteries started to make juniper tincture. They claimed it to have miraculous medical effects. The Netherlands and Belgium monks used this tincture to treat the common cold.
The British tasted genever (the name of the Dutch gin) probably sometime in the 17th century, and the story of the London Dry Gin began.
For a very long time, gin was used in its pure form as a medicine to treat gastrointestinal infections and seasickness. Gin tonic was made to treat malaria for the Britts in India. The quinine tonic itself tasted terrible, so gin was used to add flavor.
