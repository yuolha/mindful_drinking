Ale Beer

Today we have many sub-styles of ale, like pale ales, porters, and stouts. Ales are the oldest type of beer we know, and only after about the 16th century A.D. lager brewing technology was invented.

Originally ale was brewed without hops, using spices, herbs, and even fruits, although nowadays, manufacturers add hops to the recipe to label the final product as a beer. If you want to taste the “old school” ale today, you’ll have to try to find one, but still, some enthusiasts stick to traditions and make gruits (ales made with herbs). Here are some herbs you may find in a modern gruit and a brief description of their essences:

• Pine (Pinus sylvestris) symbolizes someone with a broken pride, deep regret, apology, and obsession with guilt. Due to extremely high ideals, such a person may feel burdened and depressed, needing rebirth.
• Lingonberry (Vaccinium vitis-idaea) may assist those feeling lost and punished for losing their dignity. Guilt from infringing morals.
• Cranberry (Oxycóccus) may support those defending their families, standing up for their happy lives.
• Dutch myrtle (Myrica gale) deals with illusions regarding power. It helps to understand that life gains its taste and colors not from exercising one’s authority but from other things. A Dutch myrtle is a person in search of their glory.
• Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) represents Achilles — someone with a weak point. Often this weakness is rooted in a lack of inner strength, an illusion one has to be always ready for everything.
• Birch (Betula) is in search of order in chaos. It’s someone feeling rejected, ignored, and hurt. Someone who feels different from the closest group of people. Someone in search of not knowing what. Someone who needs to ripen to resolve problems calmly and creatively.
• Horehound (Ballóta) has to decide to follow someone’s steps or go their way, be an outcast, or adopt social norms.
• Wormwood is already our well-known herb from absinthe and vermouth. It is a loner, immersed in themself, avoiding contact with others, with a history of abuse and suppression.
• Elderberry (Sambúcus nígra), which defines the Sambuca liqueur — is about unrequited love or love in which a person fears losing their freedom; it’s about disappointment and loneliness.

Ale is a top-fermented drink. These drinks remove toxins accumulated in the body due to excessive emotions. It is a treatment for anger, but it is different from rum. Rum relaxes the body, as you already know, and calms the psyche, while top-fermented beer absorbs the physical aftermath of stress and purges your body from waste.

Blessings of your heart, you brew good ale. (William Shakespeare)
