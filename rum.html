Rum

...

If cognac is an example of refinement and grace, then rum is perceived as a reckless drink devoid of timidness, bumpy, cheerful, and daring. And no wonder because the history of rum is the story of pirates and treasure hunters.
There are several versions of the origin of the name "rum":
• it is the ending of the word "saccarum,” which means "sugarcane" in Latin
• it is the beginning of the word "rumballion" (big noise, fight)
• from the name of large glasses for alcohol used by Dutch sailors — "rummers"
• from the Danish word "roemer" (glass)
• an abbreviation of the French word "arome" (aroma).

The fermentation of sugarcane has been known since ancient times, but the rum known to us was made on Barbados plantations in the 17th century, as it seems.

Rum became a drink of sailors because beer and wine got spoiled during the voyage. A spoilage-resistant disinfectant and a strong drink were needed. But not only rum can be stored for a long time. What is so special about it that made it so iconic sailor-pirate-ish?

Sugarcane (Sáccharum officinárum), the main component of rum, determined the sailor’s choice of those times because its essence is repressed anger and malice with immoral impulses. Also, rum can relax the body, leaving the head clear and able to think.

So, back to the sailors. Sailing was backbreaking work back then, so fatigue and discontent accumulated in the men’s team. Limited by the conditions of the ship, they had to hang out at sea for months. Levels of anger and aggression could go off the scale. Rum became their "psychotherapist.”

Rum is a perfect drink for anger management when someone has no other means or skills to deal with that problem.

Rum is tonic that clarifies the vision, and sets things in true perspective. (Ambrose Bierce)

When I arrived in Portugal after the start of the war in Ukraine, I was tired and angry, full of hatred towards Russians, who had invaded my homeland. A glass of Portuguese poncha made me sit down, relax, and become more calm and helped me process what had happened. In situations of seething anger and, severe physical fatigue, the inability to relax, rum is like a deep breath, like an emergency therapist.

Poncha is made of rum, honey, and fresh juice (predominantly oranges or tropical fruits). It is an excellent remedy for a sore throat. The throat sometimes hurts when we cannot deal with our anger and say what we mean.

I drank poncha slowly, remaining aware of the reactions of my body and mind. Drinking slowly helps to understand when to stop consuming the drink. When enough is enough.

By the way, the more tension there is, the less rum you need to feel better. Homeopathy is the magic of microdoses.
